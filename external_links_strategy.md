## External Linking Best Practices

### DO:
- ✅ Link to official government sites (INCIBE, AEPD)
- ✅ Link to standards organizations (NIST, OWASP, CIS)
- ✅ Link when text naturally references the resource
- ✅ Use descriptive anchor text (not "click here")
- ✅ Open in new tab (target="_blank")
- ✅ Link to original sources, not secondary articles

### DON'T:
- ❌ Use external links for internal topics (link internally instead)
- ❌ Add links just to add links (only when relevant)
- ❌ Link to competitor cybersecurity services
- ❌ Link to low-quality or unknown sources
- ❌ Create link spam (10+ external links per post is excessive)
- ❌ Link to pages that may disappear (use archive.org for citations)

## SEO Benefits

### Authority Signals
- 🔗 Linking to authoritative sites signals trust to Google
- 🔗 External links from official sources improve topical relevance
- 🔗 Government/standards sites carry high authority weight

### Ranking Factors
- ✅ Pages with diverse, relevant external links rank better
- ✅ Reduces "islands of content" (better information flow)
- ✅ Signals comprehensiveness (addressing all angles)

### User Trust
- ✅ External links to official sources build reader confidence
- ✅ Shows you've done research (not just opinion)
- ✅ Provides verification path for claims

---

## Implementation Notes

1. **Audit first**: Scan posts for claims that need source verification
2. **Link naturally**: Add external links where text naturally references the resource
3. **Use descriptive anchors**: "According to INCIBE..." not "click here"
4. **Open in new tab**: Add `target="_blank" rel="noopener"` to external links
5. **Test links**: Verify links don't break or redirect
6. **Balance**: 2-4 external links per 1500-word post is ideal

---

## Anchor Text Examples

**Good examples:**
- "According to [INCIBE's cybersecurity resources](url)"
- "The [NIST Cybersecurity Framework](url) recommends..."
- "See the [AEPD's official guidance](url) on GDPR"
- "[OWASP Top 10](url) lists this as a critical vulnerability"

**Avoid:**
- "Read more here"
- "Click this link"
- "According to [this website](url)"
- Exact match keywords as anchor text (too SEO-y)
